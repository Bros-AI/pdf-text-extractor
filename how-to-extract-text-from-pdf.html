How to extract text from a PDF (3 easy steps)

Whether your PDF is a report exported from Word or a photocopied contract scanned years ago, you can pull all its text out in under two minutes — for free, without installing anything, and without uploading your document anywhere.

Step 1 — Drop your PDF into the extractor

Open the PDF Text Extractor and drag & drop your file onto the page. You can also click to browse, or paste a PDF straight from your clipboard with Ctrl+V. Need to process several documents? Drop them all at once.

Step 2 — Let the hybrid extraction run

The tool reads each page's native text layer first — that part is instant. When a page turns out to be a scan (no usable text layer), it's automatically rendered at high resolution and recognized with OCR. A live modal shows the page being scanned and the overall progress, and every OCR'd page gets a confidence score so you know how reliable the result is.

Three modes are available: Auto (recommended), Text layer only (fastest, skips OCR), and Force OCR (useful when a PDF's text layer is garbled).

Step 3 — Copy or download your text

Each page appears as a collapsible section labeled with its source (text layer or OCR). Copy everything with one click, or download the result as a .txt file or a Markdown file with page headings — handy for pasting into notes, a CMS, or an LLM prompt.

Troubleshooting

  • Password-protected PDF? Unlock it first — the tool will tell you if a file is protected.
  • Low OCR confidence? The original scan is probably low-resolution or skewed; re-scan at 300 DPI if you can, and make sure the right language is selected.
  • Garbled characters from a digital PDF? Switch to Force OCR mode to re-read the pages from pixels instead of the broken text layer.
